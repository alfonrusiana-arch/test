History

Buug was established as a municipality on June 21, 1961, through Executive Order No. 440. The name "Buug" originated from the Subanen word "bug" meaning "river bend," referring to the winding Buug River that flows through the area.

Originally part of the municipality of Malangas, Buug became an independent municipality and has since grown into a thriving commercial center in the region.

Geography

Buug is situated in the eastern part of Zamboanga Sibugay province, bounded by:

  • North: Municipality of Malangas
  • East: Municipality of Diplahan
  • South: Sibugay Bay
  • West: Municipality of Kabasalan

Demographics

As of the 2020 census, Buug has a population of 38,000+ residents across 27 barangays. The population is predominantly Subanen, with Cebuano, Tagalog, and Ilonggo speakers also present.

Economy

The municipality's economy is primarily agricultural, with major products including:

  • Rice and Corn
  • Coconut
  • Banana
  • Fishing and Aquaculture
  • Small-scale Trading and Commerce
